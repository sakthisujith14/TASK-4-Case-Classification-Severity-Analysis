# TASK 4 - Case Classification & Severity Analysis

## Objective

Classify adverse drug reaction (ADR) cases based on seriousness and expectedness according to standard pharmacovigilance guidelines.

---

## Case Evaluation

| Case | Drug | Adverse Reaction | Seriousness | Expectedness |
|------|------|------------------|-------------|--------------|
| 01 | Amoxicillin | Skin rash, itching | Non-Serious | Expected |
| 02 | Ibuprofen | Gastrointestinal bleeding requiring hospitalization | Serious | Expected |
| 03 | Metformin | Lactic acidosis | Serious | Unexpected |
| 04 | Atorvastatin | Mild elevation of liver enzymes | Non-Serious | Expected |
| 05 | Levofloxacin | Tendon rupture | Serious | Unexpected |

---

## Summary

- **Serious Cases:** 3
- **Non-Serious Cases:** 2
- **Expected Reactions:** 3
- **Unexpected Reactions:** 2

---

## Analysis

Serious ADRs include events that result in hospitalization, are life-threatening, or cause significant medical complications. Expected reactions are already documented in the drug's safety information, while unexpected reactions are not consistent with the known product information.

Accurate classification helps regulatory authorities prioritize safety signals and ensure continuous monitoring of medicine safety.
